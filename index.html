I am currently a senior Chemistry student at the University of California, Riverside with a strong interest in analytical chemistry, forensic science, and environmental science. Throughout my time at UCR, I have developed a strong academic foundation through both classroom instruction and hands-on laboratory experience. My coursework has allowed me to gain experience in areas such as physical chemistry, instrumental analysis, spectroscopy, thermodynamics, and environmental chemistry. In laboratory settings, I have learned how to properly collect and analyze data, operate scientific instrumentation, prepare technical reports, and communicate scientific findings through presentations and written work. These experiences have strengthened my attention to detail, problem-solving abilities, and ability to work effectively both independently and in collaborative environments. I enjoy applying scientific concepts to real-world situations and continuously challenging myself to improve my technical and analytical skills through research and laboratory work.

One of the most valuable experiences during my undergraduate career has been participating in research related to forensic entomology. This project focused on monitoring insect succession patterns on decomposing animal remains in Southern California to help contribute to forensic investigations and species occurrence studies. Through this research opportunity, I gained hands-on experience collecting insect samples in field environments, organizing and interpreting data, maintaining scientific records, and helping create figures and databases for analysis. Working in research allowed me to better understand the importance of scientific accuracy, consistency, teamwork, and patience throughout the investigative process. In addition to research, I have completed multiple laboratory projects involving techniques such as UV-Vis spectroscopy, cyclic voltammetry, fluorescence spectroscopy, calorimetry, and chromatography. These experiences have also helped me become familiar with software programs such as Excel, Gaussian, WebMO, and MNova for scientific calculations, modeling, and data analysis. Each project has helped me grow more confident in my ability to adapt, learn new techniques, and solve complex scientific problems.

Outside of academics and laboratory work, I am motivated to continue developing professionally and exploring opportunities that will allow me to apply my scientific background in meaningful ways. I am particularly interested in pursuing careers related to forensic science, analytical chemistry, environmental analysis, or laboratory research where I can continue building technical experience while contributing to important scientific work. I value professionalism, communication, adaptability, and continuous learning, and I strive to bring those qualities into everything I do. Beyond science, I also enjoy learning new skills involving technology, web development, and creative problem solving, which have helped me develop a balanced mindset between technical precision and creativity. As I prepare to graduate and begin the next stage of my career, I look forward to continuing to grow both personally and professionally while contributing to work that makes a positive impact.
